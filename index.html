Section 1: Space, machines, and other stuff.

For all of my life, I have always had a facination with large, complex, things. It started with a love for railroads and trains. there was just something about them that was alluring, probably the enormous power of the locomotives, or seeing all those moving parts do their thing with perfect harmony. but it did not last forever. soon, I learned of the atomic world. A microscopic world made of infinitesimaly small peices that all fit together in different ways. this is certainly what sparred my interest in atomic reactions and processes that I still find facinating today. These interest satisfied my curiosity for a while, but something even greater was coming. SPACE!! The final frontier! It was only natural that my facination with the complex and mysterious would eventually lead me to the most intricate system of all time, the wider universe. It was almost like something clicked, something that said "This is it. This is the thing I have to be a part of." From that day onward, I can't get enough knowledge of space. This attachment to space soon led me to begin my learning journey in the field of physics, specificaly what makes the universe tick (at least on a basic scale).
